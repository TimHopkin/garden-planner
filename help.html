How to Create Your First Garden

Creating your first garden in GardenMaster is easy and intuitive. Follow these simple steps to get started:

  1. Navigate to the Garden Planner:

    Click on the "Garden Planner" tab in the navigation menu to access the planning tool.

  2. Set Your Garden Size:

    Use the "Garden Size" dropdown to select the dimensions of your garden grid (6×6, 8×8, 10×10, or 12×12).

  3. Select a Plant:

    Browse the plant list on the left side panel and click on a plant to select it.

  4. Place Plants on the Grid:

    With a plant selected, click on any cell in the garden grid to place that plant in your garden.

  5. Save Your Design:

    Once you're happy with your layout, click the "Save Garden" button to preserve your design.

Pro Tip: Pay attention to companion planting alerts that appear when placing plants. These will help you create optimal plant combinations and avoid problematic pairings.

Understanding Companion Planting

Companion planting is the practice of growing certain plants near each other for mutual benefit. GardenMaster integrates this wisdom into the planning process:

How Companion Planting Works in GardenMaster

When you place a plant on your garden grid, the system automatically checks neighboring plants for compatibility:

  • Good Companions: Plants that benefit each other through pest deterrence, pollinator attraction, or resource sharing
  • Poor Companions: Plants that may inhibit growth, compete for resources, or attract harmful pests to each other

The system will display notifications when you place a plant near either a beneficial companion or a problematic neighbor, helping you make better planting decisions.

Examples of Good Companion Pairings

  • Tomatoes + Basil: Basil repels tomato hornworms and improves tomato flavor
  • Carrots + Onions: Onion family plants repel carrot fly
  • Cucumbers + Corn: Corn provides natural trellis for cucumber vines
Pro Tip: Click on any plant in the database to view its specific companion planting recommendations in the Plant Information tab.
